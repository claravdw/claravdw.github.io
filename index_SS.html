Sunday School

High-quality, differentiated comedy courses focusing on a range of modes and skills. Classes are led by some of the best improv teachers in Boston and the world.

Why Sunday School?

You've had improv training and want to continue to improve through specific and personal notes. Whether it's with an eye to being cast on a local theater's team or just to hone your skills, our Personal Diagnostic class will keep you growing toward your goal!

Your indie team is looking to polish a form or style in order to put up an independent show or get picked up for a run/slot at a local theater. Our Group Diagnostic will provide powerful and specific direction in moving forward as a group, and to teach everyone how to work well with any ensemble!

You've always wanted to work with one of our incredible instructors and never had the chance. Many of our instructors teach exclusively at Sunday School, which means a one-of-a kind experience you can't get anywhere else!

You want to learn about the principles and skills our teachers are most excited to teach! Because our Skill Building classes are built by teachers around their interests and strengths, and our diagnostic classes allow our teachers the flexibility to teach based on their skill-set, you can count on the passion and expertise behind every class.

Programs

PIP Individual Diagnostic

An ongoing class with weekly themes so that you can hone your skills in the areas you most want to improve. Get direct and clear personal feedback on your work and specific and actionable notes to grow as a performer.

Sundays 11-1 ♦ $15 per class ♦ Max 15 people

PIP Group Diagnostic

Working with a practice group or indie team? Sign up for a class together that's tailored to your needs and concerns as a group. Our instructors will provide you with specific notes from everything from skills to team dynamic, and will provide you with ideas for exercises and forms that will help your team grow together and as individuals. Or sign up as an individual and learn skills you can use to form or work with an ensemble.

Sundays 2-4 ♦ $15 per class ♦ Max 15 people

Improv Skill Building

Each month, Sunday School will offer skills-specific workshops in a skill essential to building your repertoire as an improviser. Students must pre-register for the full month of Skill Building classes to ensure consistency throughout the session. Skill Building classes need not be taken in order, but may have prerequisites.

Sundays 2-4 ♦ $75 for 4 weeks ♦ Max 15 people

Team

Jackie Arko

Lead Instructor

Jackie Arko has been doing, teaching, directing, and producing comedy almost nonstop since 2007. She is currently a cast member of the Harold team Maxitor and the director of the Harold team BANKWOLF, both of which can be seen regularly at ImprovBoston's Harold Night. She is also a Harold instructor at ImprovBoston's Comedy School, where she has been teaching since 2011. In addition, she proudly co-produces The Kerfuffle, a monthly variety show that has quickly become a Boston favorite.Jackie is a proud Akron, Ohio native and has a special place in her heart for the Harold, Sanford Meisner, and bourbon.

     

Will Luera

Lead Instructor

Will is the Artistic Director Emeritus of ImprovBoston and also serves as an Instructor, Director and sometime performer. He joined ImprovBoston in 1997 as a Mainstage cast members and served as Artistic Director from 2000 - 2012. In addition to his work at ImprovBoston, he works with other local theaters including ImprovAsylum, Another Country Productions, Longwood Players, and The Footlight Club. He also serves as the Artistic Director of the Pilsen Improv Festival, Artistic Associate of the Chicago Improv Festival, Artistic Adviser for the Women in Comedy Festival and part-time faculty at the Boston College Theatre Department. Will is also a founder and player with Big Bang Improv.

     

David Morse

PIP Producer

David is a graduate of ImprovBoston and Improv Asylum's improv programs and has trained at Second City for improv, musical improv and sketch. He has performed in ImprovBoston shows BeatBox, Harmondo, Spooked to Soon, Studio 40, Comedy Lab and Cagematch and was a member of the indie team Murder Toad. He has an improv blog Improvana that he needs to update more often.
